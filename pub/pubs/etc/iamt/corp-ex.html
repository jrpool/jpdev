Hindi original

भारतीय अर्थव्यवस्था में और सुधार की उम्मीद

भारत की प्रमुख व्यापार संस्था भारतीय व्यापार संघ ने अच्छे मानसून और फ़सल के बाद 2003 के आर्थिक प्रगति के अपने अनुमान को और बढ़ा दिया है. भारतीय उद्योग परिसंघ यानी सीआईआई ने कहा कि इस साल भारतीय अर्थव्यवस्था में 7.2 प्रतिशत की दर से विकास होगा. जबकि इसके पहले सीआईआई ने विकास की दर 6.5 से 6.8 प्रतिशत के बीच बताई थी. सीआईआई का कहना है कि अच्छे मॉनसून के कारण अच्छी फ़सल हुई और इसका अर्थव्यवस्था पर अच्छा असर पड़ा है. पिछले साल सूखे के कारण भारतीय अर्थव्यवस्था की विकास दर अनुमान से एक प्रतिशत कम रही थी. सीआईआई के अलावा भारतीय रिज़र्व बैंक ने भी विकास दर के 6.5 से 7 प्रतिशत तक रहने का अनुमान लगाया है. सीआईआई ने सरकार से आर्थिक सुधार जारी रखने और ढांचागत क्षेत्र में निवेश कार्यक्रम को जारी रखने का अनुरोध किया है ताकि विकास दर को बनाया रखा जा सके. पिछले दो वर्षों से भारत की विकास दर पाँच प्रतिशत चल रही थी. 1991 के आर्थिक सुधार कार्यक्रमों के बाद से भारत एक आर्थिक शक्ति के रूप में उभर कर सामने आ रहा है.

English translation

More Improvements Expected in the Indian Economy

India's principal trade organization, the Indian Trade Organization has increased its estimation for the growth rate of 2003 after the harvest and the good monsoons. The Confederation of Indian Industries or the CII has also said that this year India's economy will witness a growth rate of 7.2 per cent. Initially the estimate given by the CII was between 6.5 and 6.8 per cent. CII says that due to good monsoons, there has been a good harvest, which has influenced the Indian economy in a positive manner. Last year, due to a famine, the growth rate had been 1 per cent lesser than the estimated growth rate. Apart from the CII even the Reserve Bank of India has estimated that the growth rate would remain between 6.5 and 7 per cent. CII has requested the government to continue to invest in the economic reforms and the infrastructure needs of India, so that the growth percentage can be maintained. For the past two years, India's growth rate has been at 5 per cent. After the Economic Reform Programme of 1991, India has come forward as an economically strong nation.

Another English translation

Hope of Improvement in the Indian Economy

India's major Industrial Organisation, the Confederation of Indian Industries (CII), has after the good monsoon and buoyant growth in agriculture after 2003, increased its expectation of economic growth. The CII stated that this year, India's economy would grow by 7.2%. The CII had earlier forecast that the economic growth would be between 6.5 and 6.8%. The CII stated that the good monsoon and subsequent good harvest had a positive impact on India's economy. Last year due to drought conditions, India's economy grew at a rate 1% less then estimated. The Reserve Bank of India has also estimated a growth rate of between 6.5 and 7.0% - in good agreement with CII's forecast. The CII has requested the Indian government to continue its economic reforms, along with investment in infrastructure projects, so that the inflation rate could be maintained. For the last two years the inflation rate has been 2%. Due to economic reforms initiated in 1991, India is emerging as an economic power.
