Accessibility of VEIGX web pages

Jonathan Pool

Introduction

The following table reports the results of executing the p03c13 accessibility test battery of the Autotest application on web pages of the companies in the Vanguard Global ESG Select Stock Fund (ticker symbol VEIGX).

Accessibility is a set of standards and techniques for ensuring that users of various kinds—including users with disabilities—can successfully interact with websites, mobile apps, and digital documents.

Mantaining an accessible website is one expression of a commitment to social equity. Vanguard states that its choice of companies for this fund gives greater exposure to companies with attractive environmental, social, and governance characteristics. It is therefore reasonable to expect these companies to make their websites accessible.

Findings

The tests include three external packages of tests: Axe, IBM Accessibility Checker, and WAVE. In addition, there are thirteen accessibility tests defined in Autotest, chiefly testing aspects of accessibility that the test packages do not cover.

For the main page of each company’s website, Autotest generated a deficit score. A score of 0 indicates that a page has passed all the tests. Any deficit score greater than 0 indicates that at least one test has found at least one fault on the page.

The tests were conducted in October 2021.
